The markers provided offer a good, but not perfect, match for Kupffer cells, which are the resident macrophages of the liver. **CD163 (Scavenger Receptor Cysteine-Rich Domain Family M Member 1):** CD163 is a widely accepted and highly expressed marker for mature macrophages, including Kupffer cells in the human liver. Its expression indicates the presence of cells from the monocyte-macrophage lineage. **MARCO (Macrophage Receptor with Collagenous Structure):** MARCO is another scavenger receptor expressed by certain populations of macrophages, including some Kupffer cells. Its presence further supports the identification of cells as macrophages. While not as universally expressed as CD163 across all Kupffer cell subsets, it is a significant indicator. **ALB (Albumin) and CYP3A4 (Cytochrome P450 Family 3 Subfamily A Member 4):** These markers are overwhelmingly expressed by hepatocytes and are fundamentally absent or expressed at negligible levels in Kupffer cells. The *absence* of these hepatocyte-specific markers, in conjunction with the presence of macrophage-specific markers (CD163, MARCO), is crucial for positively identifying Kupffer cells and distinguishing them from the dominant hepatocyte population. If these markers were present, it would indicate significant cellular contamination or a misidentification. Their absence, therefore, strengthens the confidence in identifying Kupffer cells based on the other markers.
