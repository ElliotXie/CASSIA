 This set of markers is highly specific for regulatory T cells (Tregs). FOXP3 is the master transcription factor for Tregs. IL2RA (CD25) is the alpha chain of the IL-2 receptor, constitutively expressed at high levels on Tregs. CTLA4, TIGIT, and ICOS are inhibitory or co-stimulatory receptors crucial for Treg function and immune suppression. TNFRSF18 (GITR) and IKZF2 (Helios) are also associated with Treg identity and stability. This combination unequivocally points to a suppressive T cell phenotype.

 This marker set is characteristic of Natural Killer (NK) cells. GNLY (Granulysin) and NKG7 are cytotoxic granule components, similar to those in CTLs but highly expressed in NK cells. KLRF1 (NKG2D) is an activating receptor on NK cells. FCER1G and TYROBP are adaptor proteins involved in signaling downstream of activating receptors on NK cells. FCGR3A (CD16) is a low-affinity Fc receptor that enables NK cells to perform antibody-dependent cell-mediated cytotoxicity (ADCC). CD38 can be expressed on activated NK cells. The presence of CD16 along with cytotoxic markers is a strong indicator of NK cells.
