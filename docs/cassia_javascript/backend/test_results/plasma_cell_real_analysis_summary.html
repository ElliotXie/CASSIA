CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Breadth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes the iterative gene expression analysis performed by CASSIA to annotate a specific cell cluster. The analysis involved two iterations, examining a total of 35 unique genes.

Initial Assessment

The initial assessment suggested a plasma cell or B cell lineage due to the strong expression of immunoglobulin-related genes: IGLL5, IGLV6-57, JCHAIN, and IGLC3. These markers are indicative of immunoglobulin production, pointing towards a terminally differentiated B cell, likely a plasma cell. However, further distinction from activated B cells or plasmablasts was deemed necessary.

Iteration 1

Hypotheses

1. To differentiate between mature B cells, plasmablasts, and plasma cells by checking canonical B cell markers (CD79A, CD19, MS4A1) and plasma cell markers (SDC1, IGHG1, IGHA1, CD27).
2. To further confirm plasma cell identity and assess activation/differentiation state by checking transcription factors and proteins critical for plasma cell differentiation and function (MZB1, XBP1, PRDM1, IRF4).
3. To assess the proliferative status of the cells, given the possibility of a transitional state or plasmablasts, by checking proliferation markers (MKI67, PCNA, TOP2A).

Key Findings

All 14 genes checked in this iteration were not present in the differential expression list. This absence significantly limited the ability to confidently distinguish between B cells, plasmablasts, and mature plasma cells, leading to significant ambiguity.

Iteration 2

Hypotheses

1. To rule out other major immune and epithelial cell types that might express non-specific genes or be present as contaminants, by checking pan T-cell (CD3E), myeloid (CD14, FCGR3A, CD68), epithelial (EPCAM), and pan-leukocyte (PTPRC) markers.
2. To further refine the B cell lineage assessment in the absence of primary B cell markers, by looking at other B cell-associated markers (CD24, CD38, CR2, CD1C, CD11C, FCER2).
3. To investigate the expression of other immunoglobulin chains (IGHM, IGHD, IGKC) to understand the B cell differentiation state and light chain usage.

Key Findings

All 15 genes checked in this iteration were not present in the differential expression list. This repeated absence of key cell type-specific markers, including those for general immune cells, B cells, and other immunoglobulin chains, severely compromised the ability to confidently annotate the cluster.

Final Annotation

The final cell type is **Immunoglobulin-producing cell (likely Plasma Cell or Plasmablast)**. Confidence level: **Low**. Key markers supporting this conclusion: IGLL5, IGLV6-57, JCHAIN, IGLC3. These markers are all directly involved in immunoglobulin production and assembly, strongly indicating a cell type dedicated to this function.

Key Marker Genes

**Immunoglobulin Production & Assembly:** IGLL5 (Immunoglobulin Lambda-Like Polypeptide 5) IGLV6-57 (Immunoglobulin Lambda Variable 6-57) JCHAIN (Joining Chain of Immunoglobulin) IGLC3 (Immunoglobulin Lambda Constant 3)

Recommendations

The user should attempt to obtain expression data for the previously requested key markers (e.g., SDC1, CD19, MS4A1, MKI67, XBP1, PRDM1, IGHG1, IGHA1, CD3E, CD14, EPCAM). Without this critical information, any annotation remains speculative. If obtaining additional gene expression data is not feasible, the user should consider this cluster as "unresolved" or "putative immunoglobulin-producing cells" and acknowledge the high uncertainty in their downstream analysis. Further experimental validation (e.g., flow cytometry or immunofluorescence) targeting these markers on sorted cells from this cluster would be necessary for a definitive annotation.
