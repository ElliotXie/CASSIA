CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Breadth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes an iterative gene expression analysis performed by CASSIA to annotate a cell cluster. The analysis involved 3 iterations and examined a total of 29 unique genes.

Initial Assessment

The initial cell type hypothesis was "Plasma Cell," strongly supported by a robust signature of immunoglobulin genes (e.g., IGHA1, IGHA2) and canonical plasma cell markers such as JCHAIN, TNFRSF17, and MZB1. The specific enrichment of IgA-related genes and CCR10 further refined this to IgA-secreting plasma cells, consistent with a large intestine origin. The presence of MYL2 was noted as an anomaly requiring further investigation.

Iteration 1

Hypotheses

1. To investigate the unexpected presence of MYL2, checking for potential contamination or doublet events involving muscle-related cells (using smooth muscle markers like ACTA2, DES) or other immune cells (T-cell marker CD3G, B-cell marker CD19).
2. To confirm the differentiation state of plasma cells and exclude less mature B-cell populations, by checking key transcription factors for plasma cell differentiation (XBP1, PRDM1, IRF4) and canonical plasma cell surface markers (CD27, CD38), while ruling out B-cell markers (CD20, PAX5).
3. To characterize the immunoglobulin isotype heterogeneity by quantifying relative expression levels of different heavy chain isotypes (IGHA1, IGHA2, IGHG1, IGHG3, IGHM) and checking for PIGR to support a mucosal IgA-secreting phenotype, along with the pan-plasma cell marker CD138.

Iteration 2

Hypotheses

1. To further investigate the MYL2 anomaly, re-evaluating the muscle cell hypothesis with more specific smooth muscle markers (ACTA2, DES, TAGLN) and considering endothelial cell doublets (CDH5, PECAM1), to determine if MYL2-expressing cells co-express other non-immune cell markers.
2. To clarify the differentiation stage given the low PRDM1 and IRF4 expression despite high XBP1, by re-examining these transcription factors and including CD138 (SDC1), a definitive plasma cell surface marker.
3. To understand the relative proportions and co-expression patterns of the confirmed multiple immunoglobulin isotypes (IGHA1, IGHA2, IGHG1, IGHG3, IGHM), and re-evaluate PIGR expression, as its low level was unexpected for mucosal IgA plasma cells.

Iteration 3

Hypotheses

1. To further investigate the MYL2 anomaly, exploring more specific markers for pericytes or other mesenchymal cells (ACTG2, CNN1, MYH11, RGS5, PDGFRB) that might co-express MYL2 and form doublets, given the lack of evidence for common muscle/endothelial markers.
2. To clarify the differentiation stage given the low SDC1, PRDM1, and IRF4 despite high XBP1, by confirming the expression of TNFRSF17 (BCMA), a robust plasma cell marker, to support plasma cell identity and potentially indicate a specific subtype or differentiation stage.
3. To determine if the mixed immunoglobulin isotypes represent distinct subpopulations or co-expression, and to confirm if PIGR is truly absent or just very low, and if its expression correlates with any specific isotype.

Final Annotation

The final cell type is **Plasma Cells**. The confidence level is **high**. This conclusion is strongly supported by the overwhelming expression of key plasma cell markers including **JCHAIN**, **TNFRSF17 (BCMA)**, **XBP1**, **CD27**, and **CD38**. The presence of multiple immunoglobulin heavy chains (IGHA1, IGHA2, IGHG1, IGHG3, IGHM) indicates a diverse population of plasma cells, consistent with the large intestine environment. The co-expression of **MYL2** and **CNN1** in a small subset of cells points to a minor doublet population involving smooth muscle cells, which should be filtered out during downstream analysis. The low expression of PRDM1, IRF4, and SDC1, while notable, does not negate the plasma cell identity given the strong positive markers, but rather suggests a specific differentiation stage or functional subtype within the plasma cell lineage.

Recommendations

The MYL2/CNN1 co-expressing subpopulation should be filtered out during downstream analysis to remove potential smooth muscle cell doublets. Further investigation into the specific differentiation stage or functional subtype of these plasma cells is warranted, given the high XBP1 and TNFRSF17 but low PRDM1, IRF4, and SDC1 expression.
