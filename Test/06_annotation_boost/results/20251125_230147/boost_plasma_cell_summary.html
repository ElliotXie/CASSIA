CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Breadth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes an iterative gene expression analysis performed by CASSIA to annotate a specific cell cluster. The analysis involved 3 iterations, examining a total of 39 unique genes.

Initial Assessment

The initial cell type hypothesis was "Plasma Cell," strongly supported by immunoglobulin genes and established plasma cell markers (JCHAIN, TNFRSF17, MZB1, GPRC5D). The presence of IgA-specific markers (IGHA1/IGHA2, JCHAIN, CCR10) suggested mucosal IgA-secreting plasma cells, consistent with a large intestine context. However, the detection of MYL2 was an outlier, raising concerns about potential contamination or doublets.

Iteration 1

Hypotheses

1. To investigate if MYL2 expression indicates smooth muscle contamination or doublets, by checking for co-expression with a canonical smooth muscle marker (ACTA2) and pan-T/B cell markers (CD3D, CD19).
2. To confirm the mature plasma cell state and rule out contamination by plasmablasts or activated B cells, by checking for high expression of plasma cell differentiation regulators (XBP1, PRDM1, IRF4) and surface markers (CD27, CD38), and low/absent B cell markers (CD20, PAX5).
3. To characterize the heterogeneity of the plasma cell population by examining relative expression levels of heavy chain isotypes (IGHG1, IGHG3, IGHA1, IGHA2, IGHM) and light chains (IGLC3, IGLC2, IGKC).

Key Findings

1. High expression of CD27 (avg_log2FC 3.91, pct.1 0.81), CD38 (avg_log2FC 4.69, pct.1 0.46), and XBP1 (avg_log2FC 1.17, pct.1 0.94) supports a mature plasma cell identity. CD3D was low (avg_log2FC -4.96, pct.1 0.03). ACTA2, CD19, CD20, and PAX5 were not in the differential expression list.
2. MYL2 showed high expression (avg_log2FC 8.15) in a small percentage of cells (12%), which remains a concern for contamination or doublets.
3. Immunoglobulin isotype analysis revealed strong signals for IgA (IGHA2 avg_log2FC 7.38, pct.1 0.89), notable presence of IgG (IGHG1 avg_log2FC 8.60, pct.1 0.12; IGHG3 avg_log2FC 7.94, pct.1 0.12), and IgM (IGHM avg_log2FC 7.69, pct.1 0.21). Both kappa (IGKC avg_log2FC -3.90, pct.1 0.80) and lambda (IGLC2 avg_log2FC 9.12, pct.1 0.88; IGLC3 avg_log2FC 9.17, pct.1 0.48) light chains were detected.
4. Low expression of IRF4 (avg_log2FC -3.84, pct.1 0.01) and PRDM1 (avg_log2FC -3.32, pct.1 0.02) was unexpected for fully differentiated plasma cells.

Iteration 2

Hypotheses

1. To further investigate MYL2 expression for smooth muscle contamination (ACTA2, DES) or macrophage doublets (CD68).
2. To re-evaluate the expression of IRF4 and PRDM1 and assess the proliferative status (MKI67, PCNA) to clarify the differentiation state (plasmablast vs. mature plasma cell).
3. To solidify the plasma cell annotation by examining additional canonical surface markers (CD138/SDC1) and definitively ruling out B cell contamination (CD19, MS4A1/CD20, CD20).

Key Findings

1. MYL2 expression (avg_log2FC 8.15, pct.1 0.12) remained high in a small fraction of cells. ACTA2, DES, and CD68 were not in the differential expression list, preventing a definitive conclusion on contamination.
2. MKI67 showed high expression (avg_log2FC 6.60, pct.1 0.61), indicating a significant proliferative component. PCNA was low (avg_log2FC -1.31, pct.1 0.09). IRF4 (avg_log2FC -3.84, pct.1 0.01) and PRDM1 (avg_log2FC -3.32, pct.1 0.02) remained low, while XBP1 (avg_log2FC 1.17, pct.1 0.94) remained high.
3. SDC1 (CD138) showed low expression (avg_log2FC -2.76, pct.1 0.01). CD19, CD20, and MS4A1 were not in the differential expression list.
4. The combination of high MKI67 and low IRF4, PRDM1, and SDC1, despite high XBP1, suggested a proliferative, less differentiated state, possibly plasmablasts.

Iteration 3

Hypotheses

1. To clarify the proliferative state by examining additional cell cycle markers (TOP2A, CDK1, CCNB1) and cell cycle inhibitors (CDKN1A, CDKN1B) to distinguish between actively dividing plasmablasts and terminally differentiated plasma cells.
2. To re-evaluate the differentiation state by confirming expression levels of key transcription factors (IRF4, PRDM1, XBP1) and surface markers (SDC1, CD27, CD38), and to obtain data for B cell markers (CD19, MS4A1) to rule out B cell contamination.
3. To comprehensively address potential contamination or doublets by obtaining expression data for ACTA2, DES, CD68, and additional macrophage/monocyte markers (CD14, FCGR3A).

Final Annotation

The final annotation for this cluster is **Plasmablasts**. **Confidence level**: Medium.

Key Marker Genes

**Immunoglobulin Genes (Antibody Secretion):** * IGHA1, IGHA2, IGHG1, IGHG3, IGHM (Heavy Chains) * IGLC2, IGLC3, IGKC (Light Chains) * JCHAIN (Polymeric Ig receptor component) * IGLL5, IGLV6-57, IGHV3-7 (Immunoglobulin Variable Regions) **Plasma Cell/Plasmablast Differentiation & Surface Markers:** * XBP1 (Master regulator of plasma cell differentiation, high) * CD27, CD38 (Upregulated during plasmablast differentiation) * IRF4, PRDM1 (Transcription factors, relatively low for mature plasma cells) * SDC1 (CD138) (Plasma cell surface marker, relatively low) **Proliferation Markers:** * MKI67, TOP2A, CDK1, CCNB1 (Indicate active cell division) * CDKN1A (Cell cycle inhibitor, unusually high) **Contamination Markers:** * MYL2 (Muscle cell marker, detected in a subset) * CD14 (Monocyte/macrophage marker, detected in a subset)

Recommendations

1. **Re-clustering and doublet removal**: The user should perform a rigorous re-clustering analysis, potentially using more stringent parameters, and apply robust doublet detection and removal algorithms to address the significant contamination indicated by CD14 and MYL2. 2. **Expression of missing markers**: It is critical to obtain expression data for the missing canonical markers: ACTA2, DES (for muscle contamination), CD68, FCGR3A (for macrophage contamination), and crucially, CD19, MS4A1 (CD20) (to definitively rule out B cell contamination and further confirm plasmablast identity). 3. **Subpopulation analysis**: If contamination issues are resolved, further sub-clustering might be warranted to investigate heterogeneity within the plasmablast population, especially concerning different immunoglobulin isotypes and the unusual CDKN1A expression.
