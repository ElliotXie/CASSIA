CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Depth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes the iterative gene expression analysis of a cell cluster using CASSIA, aiming to precisely annotate its cell type. The analysis involved two iterations of gene checking, examining a total of 17 genes to refine the initial hypothesis.

Initial Assessment

The initial assessment strongly supported enteric glial cells (EGCs) with myelinating or myelin-like properties, based on markers such as *PLP1*, *SOX10*, and *L1CAM*. However, the co-expression of *SOX2* and *FOXD3* suggested a potential subpopulation with progenitor or immature glial characteristics, prompting further investigation into the heterogeneity of the cluster.

Iteration 1

Hypotheses

1. To confirm if a subset of enteric glial cells exhibits progenitor/immature features marked by *SOX2* and *FOXD3*.
2. To determine if the *SOX2/FOXD3* positive cells lack mature glial markers (*GFAP*, *S100B*) and myelination markers (*MPZ*), which would support an immature state.
3. To investigate if *NGFR* (a neural crest progenitor marker) is co-expressed, further supporting the progenitor hypothesis.

Genes Checked

SOX2FOXD3NGFRGFAPS100BMPZ

Key Findings

The analysis revealed strong expression of *SOX2*, *FOXD3*, and *NGFR*, supporting progenitor features. However, *S100B* and *MPZ* were also highly expressed, which are markers of mature and myelinating glia, respectively. *GFAP* was not detected. This co-expression pattern suggested that the population was not purely progenitor, but rather a mixed state, possibly representing activated or transitioning EGCs.

Iteration 2

Hypotheses

1. To determine if the cells are activated or functionally specialized enteric glial cells, rather than pure progenitors, given the mixed immature (*SOX2/FOXD3*) and mature (*S100B/MPZ*) features.
2. To check for markers of activation/dedifferentiation such as *ASCL1*, *OLIG2*, *CDKN1A*, *GFRA3*, and *EDNRB*.
3. To exclude neuronal/neuroendocrine contamination by checking for *CHGA*.

Genes Checked

ASCL1OLIG2CDKN1AGFRA3EDNRBCHGA

Key Findings

The results showed expression of *CDKN1A* and *EDNRB*, supporting an activated or transitioning state. *ASCL1* and *OLIG2* were not detected, arguing against an active neuroglial progenitor state. Critically, *CHGA* was highly expressed in a significant subset, indicating contamination from neuroendocrine cells. This confirmed the presence of activated/dedifferentiated EGCs but also highlighted a mixed population.

Final Annotation

**Enteric Glial Cells (EGCs) with Activated/Dedifferentiated Features and Neuroendocrine Contamination** Confidence Level: Medium-High Main Supporting Evidence: Strong glial identity markers (*S100B, MPZ, PLP1, SOX10, PTPRZ1*) combined with progenitor-like markers (*SOX2, FOXD3, NGFR*) and activation-associated markers (*EDNRB, CDKN1A*). The high expression of *CHGA* indicates significant neuroendocrine contamination.

Key Marker Genes

**EGC Core:** *S100B, MPZ, PLP1, SOX10, PTPRZ1* **Activation/Progenitor-like:** *SOX2, FOXD3, NGFR, EDNRB, CDKN1A* **Contamination:** *CHGA*

Recommendations

If purity is critical, subclustering is recommended to separate the *CHGA+* neuroendocrine cells from the glial population. Further functional assays are suggested to validate whether *SOX2/FOXD3+* cells represent injury-responsive glia or a stable subset.
