CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Breadth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes the iterative gene expression analysis performed by CASSIA to annotate a specific cell cluster. The analysis involved 5 iterations, examining a total of 30 unique genes to refine the cell type annotation.

Initial Assessment

The initial hypothesis was that the cluster represented Enteric Glial Cells, supported by markers like PLP1, SOX10, and PTPRZ1. However, uncertainty arose due to the presence of non-exclusively glial markers (SPP1, LPL), ambiguity regarding ENS myelination, and the expression of L1CAM, NRXN1/NRXN3 suggesting interactions with other cell types. The high expression of NKAIN3 also warranted further investigation.

Iteration 1

Hypotheses

1. To confirm glial identity and rule out hematopoietic or endothelial contamination by checking canonical glial markers (GFAP, S100B) and absence of pan-hematopoietic (CD45) and endothelial (CD34) markers.
2. To investigate myelinating vs. non-myelinating glia subtypes using specific markers (MPZ, MBP for myelinating; ERBB3, NGFR for immature/non-myelinating).
3. To explore the possibility of enteric glial progenitors or neural stem cells by checking for associated markers (SOX9, NES, PROM1, CD133).

Key Findings

High expression of S100B, MPZ, ERBB3, NES, and NGFR was observed. Low expression of MBP, PROM1, and SOX9 was noted. The absence of CD133, CD34, CD45, and GFAP from the differential expression list was highlighted as a critical gap. These findings suggested a complex glial population with both myelinating/myelin-associated and immature/non-myelinating components, with progenitor characteristics not being dominant.

3. To understand the specific contribution of initially noted non-exclusively glial markers (CDH19, L1CAM, NRXN1, NRXN3, SPP1, LPL, NKAIN3) to the phenotype of this specific glial population.

Final Annotation

The final cell type is **Enteric Glial Cells, with a significant myelin-associated and immature/progenitor component.** Confidence level: **Medium-High**

Key Marker Genes

* **General Glial Identity:** S100B, PTPRZ1, L1CAM, NRXN1, NRXN3, CDH19, CHL1, CADM2. * **Neural Crest/Developmental:** SOX10, FOXD3, SOX2, NES, NGFR. * **Myelin-Associated/Myelinating:** PLP1, MPZ, PMP22. * **Metabolic/Supportive/Reactive:** LPL, SPP1, APOE, CLU, CST3. * **Immature/Plasticity:** ERBB3.

Recommendations

The user should prioritize obtaining expression data for GFAP, KROX20, CD34, and CD45. GFAP is needed to confirm glial maturity, KROX20 for myelinating commitment, and CD34/CD45 to confirm cluster purity. Further sub-clustering based on myelin-associated (PLP1, MPZ, PMP22, KROX20) versus immature/progenitor (SOX2, NES, NGFR, ERBB3) markers is recommended to delineate distinct glial subtypes.
