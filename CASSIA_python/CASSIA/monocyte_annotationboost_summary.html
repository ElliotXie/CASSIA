CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Depth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This analysis focused on a specific cell cluster suspected to be enteric glial cells (EGCs). The analysis iteratively checked gene expression patterns to determine if there was a distinct progenitor subpopulation within the cluster or if the cells represented a transitional state. A total of 11 genes were checked across two rounds of analysis.

Initial Hypothesis

The initial hypothesis was that the cluster contained a distinct progenitor-like glial subpopulation coexisting with mature myelinating glia, based on the presence of *SOX2* and *FOXD3* alongside mature glial markers.

Gene Analysis 1: Investigating Progenitor vs. Mature Glial States

Genes Analyzed

SOX2FOXD3NGFRGFAPS100BMPZ

Findings & Conclusions

Expression of progenitor markers (*SOX2*, *FOXD3*, *NGFR*) was high, but mature glial markers (*S100B*, *MPZ*) were also highly expressed. *GFAP* was not detected. This mixed signature suggested either a transitional state or technical overlap, complicating the initial hypothesis of distinct subpopulations.

Gene Analysis 2: Resolving Ambiguity: Proliferation and Glial Lineage Markers

Genes Analyzed

MKI67ASCL1OLIG1ERBB3CDH19

Findings & Conclusions

*MKI67* expression was high, indicating active proliferation. *ERBB3* was moderately expressed, consistent with glial differentiation. *CDH19* was uniformly high, confirming neural crest origin. *ASCL1* and *OLIG1* were not detected. The co-expression of *MKI67* with mature markers (*S100B*, *MPZ* from the previous check) suggested a transitional state rather than clearly separated progenitor and mature subsets. The absence of *GFAP*, *ASCL1*, and *OLIG1* may reflect ENS-specific glial biology.

Final Conclusion

The final annotation is **Proliferative Enteric Glial Cells with Myelin-like Features**. The confidence level is Medium-High. Key supporting evidence includes the expression of glial markers (*PLP1*, *SOX10*, *S100B*, *PTPRZ1*), proliferation/progenitor markers (*MKI67*, *SOX2*, *FOXD3*), and maturation/myelin-like markers (*MPZ*, *ERBB3*).

Key Insights

The cluster represents enteric glial cells with both proliferative/progenitor features and mature/myelin-like characteristics. The co-expression of markers suggests these cells may be in a transitional state rather than forming distinct progenitor and mature subpopulations. The specific marker profile (e.g., lack of *GFAP*) may reflect unique properties of enteric glia.

Validation Status

The analysis is complete based on the available data. Remaining uncertainties include definitively distinguishing a transitional state from potential minor contaminating populations or technical artifacts. Further validation through subclustering and functional assays is recommended.
