CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Breadth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes a gene expression analysis performed by CASSIA to annotate a specific cell cluster. The analysis involved 3 iterations of hypothesis testing, examining a total of 29 unique genes.

Initial Assessment

The initial assessment of the cell cluster suggested a plausible Schwann cell identity due to strong expression of canonical myelin proteins (PMP22, MPZ, PLP1). However, the co-expression of numerous neuronal markers (NRXN1/3, GRIK3, GFRA3, SCN7A) introduced ambiguity, leading to hypotheses of a mixed population of Schwann cells and enteric neurons, a specific state of enteric glia with neuronal-like features, or a developmental stage where lineages are less distinct.

Key Findings

High expression of PMP22, MPZ, and PLP1 continued to support Schwann cell identity. High expression of S100B and SOX10 reinforced a glial, neural crest-derived identity. Notably, very high expression of CDH19 suggested a strong enteric glial component, and NES was also present. SYP, a neuronal marker, was detected but with lower average expression and percentage of cells compared to core glial markers. Key neuronal markers (TUBB3, RBFOX3, ELAVL3) and GFAP were not in the differential expression list.

Iteration 2

Hypotheses

1. To further distinguish between mature myelinating Schwann cells and non-myelinating enteric glia, given the strong myelin protein expression and high CDH19.
2. To definitively assess the presence and potential subtype of any neuronal population, as SYP was detected but other pan-neuronal markers were not.
3. To explore the possibility of immature or precursor Schwann cells, or a developmental continuum, due to co-expression of myelin markers with CDH19 and NES, and initial presence of FOXD3.

Key Findings

Continued strong expression of PMP22, MPZ, CDH19, SOX10, and S100B pointed to a glial identity. A critical finding was the negative avg_log2FC for MBP, strongly arguing against a mature myelinating Schwann cell population. High expression of SOX2, NGFR, FOXD3, and NEUROD1, along with MAP2 and SYP, suggested a population with significant neurogenic and glial precursor characteristics. The absence of other specific neuronal markers (CHAT, DCX, NOS1, VIP) and mature myelin markers (MOG, KROX20) was also noted.

Iteration 3

Hypotheses

1. To confirm the co-expression patterns of markers characteristic of immature or precursor enteric glial cells, given the high expression of SOX2, NGFR, FOXD3, CDH19, and NES, and low/absent MBP.
2. To assess if the co-expression of glial precursor markers (SOX2, NGFR, SOX10) with neuronal markers (NEUROD1, MAP2, SYP) indicates a neuro-glial progenitor population.
3. To conduct a focused check on the neuronal component to determine if a distinct subpopulation of immature/developing neurons exists.

Key Findings

Consistent high expression of SOX10, SOX2, NGFR, FOXD3, CDH19, and NES, combined with low/absent mature myelin markers (MBP, MOG) and lack of robust pan-neuronal markers (TUBB3, RBFOX3, ELAVL3, DCX), strongly indicated an immature or progenitor population. The presence of NEUROD1, MAP2, and SYP was interpreted as indicative of neuronal potential within a predominantly glial precursor context. The absence of ASCL1 further supported a broader neuro-glial developmental program over a committed neuronal lineage.

Final Annotation

The final cell type is **Enteric Neuro-Glial Progenitors / Immature Enteric Glia**. Confidence level: High. Key markers supporting this conclusion include the consistent high expression of SOX10, SOX2, NGFR, FOXD3, CDH19, and NES, which are characteristic of neural crest-derived glial precursors and immature enteric glia. The presence of NEUROD1, MAP2, and SYP, in the absence of more definitive mature neuronal markers or mature myelin markers (MBP, MOG), suggests a population with neurogenic potential or early neuronal differentiation within a predominantly glial progenitor context. The lack of GFAP further distinguishes this from mature or reactive astrocytes/glia.
