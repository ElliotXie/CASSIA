CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Breadth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes the iterative gene expression analysis performed by CASSIA to annotate a specific cell cluster. The analysis involved 1 iteration and examined a total of 15 genes.

Initial Assessment

The initial cell type hypothesis was **CD8+ Cytotoxic T Cells (NK-like/Trm phenotype)**. This was supported by core markers (CD8A/B, cytotoxic effectors) and tissue-resident features (TIGIT, LAG3). Ambiguity arose from the co-expression of NK receptors (KLRC2, KLRD1, XCL1/2) and γδ-associated TRGC1/2, suggesting possibilities of NK/γδ contamination, hybrid NK-like CD8+ T cells, or unresolved doublets. The lack of TRDV1/2 weakened the γδ hypothesis.

Iteration 1

Hypotheses

1. To distinguish NK cells from γδ T cells and rule out significant contamination.
2. To confirm the tissue-resident memory (Trm) phenotype and explain NK receptor co-expression.
3. To rule out cycling cells or doublets, supporting a homogeneous population.

Genes Checked

NCAM1FCGR3ATRDV1TRDV2ITGAECD69PDCD1HAVCR2MKI67TOP2ASTMN1

Key Findings

The absence of NCAM1, TRDV1/2, and proliferation markers (MKI67, TOP2A) ruled out significant NK/γδ contamination or doublets. High expression of ITGAE and CD69 solidified the Trm call. The co-expression of NK receptors (KLRC2, KLRD1) was consistent with a tissue-adapted subset.

Final Annotation

The cluster is confidently annotated as **CD8+ Tissue-Resident Memory T Cells (Trm) with NK-like features** (high confidence). This is supported by the presence of CD8A/B, GZMA/B/H, ITGAE, CD69, and NK receptors KLRC2/KLRD1, alongside the absence of markers for significant NK/γδ contamination or doublets.

Key Marker Genes

**Core T cell markers:** CD8A, CD8B **Cytotoxic effector markers:** GZMA, GZMB, GZMH **Tissue-resident memory markers:** ITGAE, CD69, TIGIT, LAG3 **NK-like features:** KLRC2, KLRD1, XCL1, XCL2 **Exhaustion markers (checked):** PDCD1, HAVCR2 **Proliferation markers (checked):** MKI67, TOP2A, STMN1 **NK/γδ exclusion markers (checked):** NCAM1, FCGR3A, TRDV1, TRDV2, TRGC1, TRGC2

Recommendations

TCR sequencing would be required to resolve alternative possibilities, such as rare γδ subsets, though the dominant population is clearly CD8+ Trm.
