CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Breadth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes a gene expression analysis performed by CASSIA to annotate a specific cell cluster. The analysis involved one primary iteration of hypothesis testing, examining a total of 15 genes.

Initial Assessment

The initial assessment suggested the cell cluster was composed of proliferating transit-amplifying (TA) cells, supported by strong expression of mitotic and cell cycle regulators (e.g., *TOP2A*, *AURKB*, *MKI67*). However, ambiguity arose from the lack of definitive TA-specific markers (*SMOC2*, *TSPAN8*) and the need to distinguish them from cycling intestinal stem cells (ISCs) or potential proliferating stromal/immune doublets. The absence of spatial data also limited confidence.

Iteration 1

Hypotheses

1. To distinguish TA cells from cycling ISCs, by checking for expression of known ISC markers and regulators.
2. To rule out contamination from proliferating stromal doublets, by checking for expression of stromal and immune cell markers.
3. To identify potential transitional states or early differentiation skewing, by checking for low-level expression of markers for differentiated intestinal cell types.

Key Findings

The analysis revealed an absence of ISC markers (*LGR5*, *OLFM4*) and stromal/immune markers, effectively ruling out these alternative cell types. While *SMOC2* expression was weak, no evidence supported the presence of cycling ISCs or stromal/immune doublets. The initial strong expression of mitotic regulators (*TOP2A*, *AURKB*, *MKI67*) was further supported by the mention of *PLK1* in the final conclusion, reinforcing the proliferating nature of the cells.

Final Annotation

The cluster is confidently annotated as **proliferating transit-amplifying (TA) cells** (high confidence).

Key Marker Genes

Recommendations

Spatial validation would further solidify the cell type annotation.
