CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes the iterative gene expression analysis performed by CASSIA to annotate a cell cluster from the large intestine. The analysis involved 4 iterations, examining a total of 48 unique genes.

Initial Assessment

The initial hypothesis was Myelinating Schwann Cells, supported by the presence of PLP1, SOX10, and FOXD3. However, the presence of markers associated with broader neuron-glia interactions (L1CAM, NRXN1, CADM2, PTPRZ1) and other glial markers (GFRA2, CHADL, CHST9, GJC3), along with the large intestine context, suggested potential alternative glial subtypes or mixed populations.

Iteration 1

Hypotheses

1. To confirm the myelinating phenotype and distinguish from other glial cells by examining canonical myelin markers and markers of other glial types.
2. To differentiate from Non-myelinating (Remak) Schwann cells by checking for markers typically higher in non-myelinating cells and other associated markers.
3. To rule out potential contamination or doublets with other common stromal or epithelial cell types present in the large intestine.

Final Annotation

The final cell type is **Enteric Glial Cells (Mixed Myelinating and Non-myelinating)**. The confidence level is high. Key supporting evidence includes the presence of pan-glial markers (SOX10, S100B), myelinating markers (PLP1, MPZ, PMP22, EGR2), and the non-myelinating marker (NGFR), with co-expression patterns indicating heterogeneity.

Key Marker Genes

Pan-Glial Markers: SOX10, S100B, NCAM1 Myelinating Schwann Cell Markers: PLP1, MPZ, PMP22, EGR2, PRX Non-myelinating Schwann Cell Markers: NGFR Neuron-Glia Interaction Markers: NRXN1, L1CAM, CADM2, PTPRZ1 Other Highly Expressed/Potential Subtype Markers: CDH19, LPL, SFRP5, MEGF10, CDH2, TENM3, FAM19A5, GFRA2, CHADL, CHST9, GJC3, FGFBP2, LINC01436 Markers Indicating Heterogeneity/Transitional States: SOX2, CRTAC1, CHL1, GRIK3, TUBB2B, CMTM5, ZNF536

Recommendations

Further analysis at the single-cell level, such as subclustering or trajectory inference, is recommended to fully characterize the heterogeneity, resolve distinct subpopulations (myelinating vs. non-myelinating), and identify potential transitional states within this cluster.
