CASSIA Cell Type Annotation Summary (Breadth-First Analysis)

Single-cell RNA-seq Analysis Report

Overview

This report summarizes the iterative gene expression analysis performed by CASSIA to annotate a specific cell cluster. The analysis involved 5 iterations, examining a total of 55 unique genes to refine the cell type annotation.

Initial Assessment

The initial hypothesis for this cluster was Natural Killer (NK) cells, supported by strong expression of NK-specific markers (KLRC2, KLRK1, KLRC4, KLRD1, XCL2, XCL1, CRTAM, CD160) and general cytotoxic markers (Granzymes, PRF1, NKG7). However, co-expression of CD8A/B and TRGC1/2, along with shared cytotoxic markers, introduced ambiguity, suggesting potential mixed populations of CD8+ Cytotoxic T Lymphocytes (CTLs) or Gamma-Delta (γδ) T cells, a specific NK cell subset with T-cell like features, or a transitional state.

Iteration 1

Hypotheses

1. To distinguish between NK cells and T cells by assessing pan-T cell markers (CD3E, TRAC, TRBC1, TRBC2) and additional definitive NK cell markers (KLRF1, NCR1, NCR3).
2. To investigate the potential for CD4+ T cells and specific T cell subsets (CD4, FOXP3, IL2RA, CCR7, SELL, CD69, CD103) given the presence of CD8A/B.
3. To clarify the contribution of gamma-delta T cells using more specific markers (TRDV1, TRGV9, CD1D, BTNL3, BTNL8).

Key Findings

The results strongly refuted a significant pan-T cell population, showing very low expression and high negative log2FC for CD3E, TRAC, TRBC1, and TRBC2. CD69 was also very low. BTNL3 showed low but present expression. Crucially, many key markers (BTNL8, CCR7, CD103, CD1D, CD4, FOXP3, IL2RA, KLRF1, NCR1, NCR3, SELL, TRDV1, TRGV9) were not in the differential expression list, preventing definitive conclusions.

Iteration 2

Hypotheses

1. To definitively confirm NK cell identity by checking highly specific NK cell markers (KLRF1, NCR1, NCR3, FCGR3A, NCAM1) given the strong negative results for pan-T cell markers.
2. To further solidify the conclusion that the cluster is not a conventional T cell population by checking additional TCR complex components and signaling molecules (CD3D, CD3G, CD247, ZAP70, LCK, ITK, CD2).
3. To confirm or refute a significant gamma-delta T cell presence by assessing TRDV1, TRGV9, CD1D, and markers expressed by both NK and some T cell subsets (KLRG1, CD94, NKG2A, NKG2C).

Genes Checked

Key Findings

The data strongly reinforced the exclusion of conventional T cells, with very low or absent expression of CD2, CD247, CD3D, CD3G, ITK, and LCK. NCAM1 (CD56) showed high expression, a significant positive indicator for NK cells. ZAP70 was present but at lower levels than expected for a dominant T cell population. However, key NK-specific markers (KLRF1, NCR1, NCR3, FCGR3A) and gamma-delta T cell markers (TRDV1, TRGV9, CD1D) remained unassessed.

Iteration 3

Hypotheses

1. To definitively confirm NK cell identity by checking core NK cell markers (KLRF1, NCR1, NCR3, FCGR3A, CD7, CD56).
2. To fully resolve the gamma-delta T cell hypothesis by assessing TRDV1, TRGV9, CD1D, CD161 (KLRB1).
3. To consider the possibility of less differentiated or progenitor-like cells, or specific tissue-resident populations, by checking markers like CD34, KIT (CD117), PROM1 (CD133), CD45RA, and CD45RO.

2. To fully resolve the gamma-delta T cell hypothesis by assessing TRDV1, TRGV9, CD1D, CD161, and re-checking CD3D.
3. To characterize the NK cell population's maturation and functional state by assessing CD45RA, CD45RO, CD27, CD28, CD57, KIR2DL1, and KIR3DL1.

Iteration 5

Hypotheses

1. To obtain expression data for the most specific and robust positive markers for human NK cells (KLRF1, NCR1, NCR3, FCGR3A, CD56).
2. To definitively rule out a significant gamma-delta T cell component by assessing TRDV1, TRGV9, CD1D, CD161, and CD3E.
3. To characterize the NK cell population's maturation and functional potential by assessing CD57, KIR2DL1, KIR3DL1, NKG2A, NKG2C, NKG2D, and CD94.

Final Annotation

The final cell type for this cluster is **Natural Killer (NK) Cells**. Confidence level: **Medium** Key markers supporting this conclusion include the initial strong presence of NK-specific markers (KLRC2, KLRK1, KLRC4, KLRD1, XCL2, XCL1, CRTAM, CD160) and general cytotoxic effector molecules (Granzymes, PRF1, NKG7). Crucially, the repeated and robust absence of pan-T cell markers (CD3E, TRAC, TRBC1, TRBC2, CD2, CD247, CD3D, CD3G, ITK, LCK) strongly argues against a conventional T cell identity, including CD8+ CTLs. The high expression of NCAM1 (CD56) in an earlier check also supports an NK cell identity.

Key Marker Genes

**Initial NK Markers:** KLRC2, KLRK1, KLRC4, KLRD1, XCL2, XCL1, CRTAM, CD160 **General Cytotoxic Markers:** Granzymes, PRF1, NKG7 **Supporting NK Markers:** NCAM1 (CD56), CD27 **Markers Excluded for T-cells:** CD3E, TRAC, TRBC1, TRBC2, CD8A, CD8B, CD2, CD247, CD3D, CD3G, ITK, LCK, CD69 **Markers with Low/Absent Expression (suggesting non-progenitor/non-gamma-delta T-cell):** BTNL3, CD7, KLRB1, PROM1

Recommendations

To achieve high confidence in the NK cell annotation, it is recommended to prioritize obtaining expression data for KLRF1, NCR1, NCR3, FCGR3A, TRDV1, and TRGV9. High expression of the NK-specific markers would solidify the NK cell annotation, while high expression of TRDV1/TRGV9 would necessitate a re-evaluation towards a gamma-delta T cell population.
